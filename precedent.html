What was wrong with v0.1
PROBLEM 1 — TWO PROJECTS IN ONE BODY

The draft tried to be both a leader psychology archive (slow, deep, research-intensive) and a real-time policy alert system (fast, agentic, news-driven). These are different tempo systems. Building them simultaneously at v0.1 means building neither well. They need to be sequenced, not merged.

PROBLEM 2 — THE 5 DIMENSIONS AREN'T EXTRACTABLE

"Digital Maturity" and "Global Alignment" are editorial judgments disguised as data fields. You cannot reliably prompt an AI to score these from a speech — the variance will be too high and the outputs won't be comparable across leaders or time. A field is only valid if two independent analysts reading the same source would score it the same way. The dimensions need to be rebuilt from that principle.

PROBLEM 3 — THE "2-HOUR UPDATE" IS OPERATIONAL FANTASY AT V0.1

A solo builder cannot maintain a real-time ingestion pipeline, validate AI extractions, curate historical twin matches, and produce a public interface simultaneously. Speed at the cost of accuracy destroys credibility. At v0.1: weekly cadence, manual validation on every AI output, quality over freshness.

PROBLEM 4 — "MENTAL MODEL" WAS UNDEFINED

The draft used the phrase but never defined what it means operationally. What fields does a mental model contain? How do you extract it from a speech? How do you update it when a leader contradicts themselves? Without a schema, the concept stays abstract forever.

What this project actually is — sharpened
THE CORE IDEA

A structured, evidence-based database of how specific leaders reason about specific problems — built by systematically analyzing their speeches, decrees, and decisions over time — then used to predict what their next policy decisions will look like and match those predictions against historical cases where similar logic was tried and either worked or failed.

A What it is NOT

It is not a promise tracker. Leaders lie, hedge, and change positions — tracking promises creates false precision over inherently vague language. A leader who keeps every promise can still destroy an economy.

It is not a real-time news aggregator. Speed without depth is noise.

It is not a scoring system. "Leader X gets 72/100" is meaningless and gameable.

B What it IS

A reasoning map. For each leader: what they believe, why they believe it, how those beliefs generate specific decisions, and what history says happens when those decisions are made.

A historical twin engine. When Cambodia makes a move, the system finds the closest structural match in global history and surfaces the outcome.

A conflict detector. When two leaders' logic contradicts — it flags it.

The problem it solves — at first principles

The deepest problem in governance is the gap between when decisions are made and when consequences arrive.

A minister announces an SEZ policy today. The consequences appear in 2031. By then: the minister may be gone, the original reasoning is forgotten, the causal chain is disputed, and the political incentive is to deny the connection. This gap is how bad decisions hide.

Cambodia has a specific version of this problem that makes it uniquely urgent: the 2029 LDC graduation is a hard deadline with no extension. EBA trade preferences end. EU market access reprices. The entire export-led growth model built since 1993 faces its first structural stress test. If the assumptions embedded in current leadership thinking are wrong — Cambodia will not know until it's too late to adapt.

This system makes those assumptions visible, comparable, and testable against history — before 2029, not after.

The mental model schema — what you actually extract

A "mental model" sounds abstract. Here it is made concrete. For each leader you extract and maintain 7 structured fields. Each field must be directly quotable from a source — no inference without evidence.

NO.
FIELD
WHAT IT CAPTURES
HOW EXTRACTED
01
Growth Theory
What does this leader believe drives economic growth? State-led investment? FDI attraction? SME development? Export manufacturing? Their answer determines every budget priority they ever make.
Direct quotes from speeches. Budget justifications. What they fund vs defund.
02
Risk Tolerance
When facing uncertainty, does this leader move fast and correct later, or wait for consensus? High or low tolerance for debt, for experiment, for short-term pain? This predicts crisis response behavior.
Decisions made under pressure. Speed of response to shocks. Debt levels approved.
03
Time Horizon
Is this leader optimizing for the next 12 months, the next political cycle, or the next 20 years? Short-horizon leaders sacrifice infrastructure for consumption. Long-horizon leaders do the reverse. Cambodia's 2029 problem requires long-horizon thinking from everyone in the room.
Infrastructure spend vs current expenditure. Stated planning windows. Project timelines approved.
04
Dependency Assumptions
What external conditions does this leader assume will hold? EBA preferences continuing? China investment sustaining? US tariffs staying low? These are the hidden load-bearing assumptions that, if wrong, collapse the entire policy architecture.
Explicit forecast statements. Budget assumptions. What they don't hedge against.
05
Institution vs Relationship
Does this leader work through formal institutions (laws, independent bodies, transparent processes) or through personal relationships and informal networks? This determines whether their decisions are reproducible or person-dependent — critical for long-term stability.
Who they meet with. How decisions are announced. Whether they build or bypass institutions.
06
Global Positioning Logic
Not "are they pro-China or pro-West" — that's too simple. Instead: what is their actual theory of how Cambodia extracts value from great power competition? Hedge and extract from both? Align with one for scale? Stay neutral and sell to everyone? The logic, not the label.
Trade agreement priorities. Which summits they attend. Investment source diversification ratios.
07
Consistency Score
Over time: how often does this leader's behavior match their stated reasoning? High consistency means the mental model is reliable for prediction. Low consistency means either the reasoning is performative or the situation is changing faster than the model. Both are important signals.
Systematic: compare stated reasoning in speeches vs actual budget/decree outcomes. Updated quarterly.

The historical twin engine — how it actually works
What a "twin" is

A historical twin is a country that faced a structurally similar situation to Cambodia's current position — same combination of: export structure, external market dependency, development stage, and leadership logic — and made a comparable policy move.

The twin isn't found by surface similarity ("both are Southeast Asian"). It's found by matching the underlying mechanism — the economic logic — not the geography or culture.

Why twins matter more than forecasts

Forecasts require perfect models of complex systems. Historical twins require only the assumption that similar mechanisms produce similar outcomes — which is far more defensible.

When you show that Cambodia's current EBA strategy matches Vietnam 2010 in 6 of 7 structural dimensions — and Vietnam's outcome at year 5 was X — that is far more credible than any econometric projection.

Example twins already identified
TRADE GRADUATION
Vietnam 2007–2015 — WTO accession + EBA usage + FDI pivot. Cambodia 2024 is structurally 60–70% similar. Vietnam outcome: successful but required 8 years of real institutional reform, not just legal reform.
SEZ STRATEGY
Bangladesh 2010–2020 — Export-led growth via garment SEZs, single-market dependency (EU), graduation pressure. Outcome: partial — retained market access but failed to diversify, now facing same pressure again.
CBDC + DOLLARIZATION
Ecuador 2014–2017 — Attempted parallel digital currency alongside USD dollarization. Outcome: failed — adoption never reached critical mass, abandoned after 3 years. Structurally similar dependency dynamic to Bakong/Riel.
How it works — the full processing pipeline
01
Ingest
Pull source documents weekly
Python scraper pulls from: PMO website, NBC publications, MEF budget docs, MoC trade reports, CDC investment data, Khmer Times English edition. Saves raw text + metadata (date, author, source URL, document type).
OUTPUT: raw_docs/{date}/{source}.txt
02
Extract
Claude reads and extracts structured fields
For each document: Claude is prompted with the 7-field mental model schema. Returns structured JSON: which leader, which fields are evidenced, exact quote supporting each, confidence level (High/Medium/Low), and what field it updates.
OUTPUT: extracted/{leader_id}/{date}.json
03
Update
Merge into leader profiles, flag changes
New extraction is compared to existing profile. Consistency score updates automatically. If a field contradicts a previous position — flagged as "Position Shift" with both data points preserved. Nothing is overwritten, everything is versioned.
OUTPUT: profiles/{leader_id}/profile_v{n}.json
04
Match
Find historical twins for new policy signals
When a significant new policy signal is detected, the Twin Engine queries the historical case library (50+ structured cases). Claude scores structural similarity across 6 dimensions. Top 3 twins returned with outcome summary and confidence rating.
OUTPUT: twins/{signal_id}/matches.json
05
Publish
Generate static site, deploy weekly
Python generates HTML from all profiles + twin matches. GitHub Actions deploys to GitHub Pages every Sunday. No server. No database in production. Total hosting cost: $0. Interface: leader profiles, conflict map, twin alerts, consistency scores.
OUTPUT: docs/ → github pages
What the output actually looks like — three artifact types
Artifact Type 01 — Leader Mental Model Profile
CORE OUTPUT

What it is: A structured one-page profile per leader. 7 fields. Each field shows: current assessed position, evidence source with date, confidence level, and change history. Not a biography — a decision-making map.

Example entry: Aun Pornmoniroth / Field 04 — Dependency Assumptions / "Budget 2026 prepared on assumption of 10% US tariff rate remaining stable through 2027" / Source: MEF Budget Circular Nov 2025 / Confidence: High / Risk Flag: Historical base rate of US tariff stability in election-adjacent years is 42%.

Who uses it: Journalists covering policy · Researchers · Investors doing country risk
Artifact Type 02 — Historical Twin Signal
HIGH VALUE

What it is: When a significant policy decision is announced, a Twin Signal is published. Shows: the Cambodia decision, the 3 closest historical matches, structural similarity score per dimension, and what the outcome was at 1/3/5 year marks in the historical case.

Example: "Cambodia's SEZ expansion logic (2026) matches Shenzhen Phase 2 (1992) at 71% structural similarity and Bangladesh EPZ expansion (2012) at 64%. Shenzhen outcome at 5 years: transformative. Bangladesh outcome at 5 years: stagnant. Key differentiating factor: institutional quality of land dispute resolution."

Who uses it: Policy researchers · Economic journalists · Development banks · Think tanks
Artifact Type 03 — Leadership Conflict Map
UNIQUE FEATURE

What it is: A visual map showing where the 10 leaders' mental models agree and where they conflict. When Cham Nimul's trade diversification logic contradicts Aun Pornmoniroth's budget assumptions — that conflict is made visible. Conflicts are often where policy breaks down in implementation.

Example conflict detected: "NBC Governor Chea Serey's de-dollarization timeline (Riel reaching 30% of transactions by 2028) is structurally incompatible with MEF's 2027 budget which assumes USD-denominated FDI inflows at current levels through 2029. These assumptions cannot both be true simultaneously."

Who uses it: Anyone trying to understand why Cambodian policy implementation repeatedly underperforms stated strategy

Why nothing existing competes with this — specifically
MOAT 1
The historical twin library is hand-built and irreplaceable. Wikipedia doesn't have 50 structured SEZ case studies with 7-dimension scoring. Academic databases do — but they're paywalled, unsearchable in real time, and not formatted for AI reasoning. Building this library slowly and correctly is the project's core asset. It compounds.
MOAT 2
The mental model schema is explicit where others are implicit. Every political commentator has a model of how leaders think. None of them have made it structured, versioned, and falsifiable. That's the difference between opinion and instrument.
MOAT 3
The conflict detection is genuinely novel. No existing tool maps internal contradictions between a government's own senior officials. This is where most policy failure actually originates — not corruption, not bad intentions, but incompatible assumptions held simultaneously by people who never directly compare notes.
MOAT 4
Cambodia's 2029 deadline creates urgency that makes the work immediately useful. This isn't a project you build and wait for relevance. Every month that passes without this instrument, decisions are being made on assumptions nobody has stress-tested. The deadline makes this actionable now.
MOAT 5
It is open, public, and free. Every think tank and consultancy doing this work sells access. This publishes everything. That makes it citable, shareable, and trusted in a way that paywalled analysis never can be.
