Methodology

The 7 Dimensions

Every leader profile maps doctrine across seven dimensions drawn from comparative political economy. These axes were selected because they predict the most consequential development-policy divergences in Southeast Asian contexts.

DimensionWhat it measuresWhy it matters
Growth theoryState-led vs market-led growth modelDetermines FDI policy, SOE reform appetite, and industrial strategy
Risk tolerancePreference for stability vs changePredicts reform pace, crisis response, and tolerance for short-term pain
Time horizonShort-cycle vs long-cycle planningExplains infrastructure choices, institution-building commitment, and election-cycle policy distortions
Dependency assumptionsView on foreign capital and multilateral conditionalityDetermines how leaders respond to EBA, IMF, ADB, and Chinese BRI trade-offs
Institution vs relationshipRule-based vs personal-network governancePredicts reform durability, anti-corruption commitment, and succession risk
Global positioning logicNon-alignment vs bloc alignmentDetermines US/China/EU triangle management and treaty compliance
Consistency scoreStated positions vs revealed behaviorDistinguishes performative from operational doctrine

Source Standards

Profiles are built exclusively from primary sources: official speeches, policy documents, budget statements, legislative records, and press conference transcripts. Each dimension entry requires all four of the following to be considered populated:

Required fieldStandard
Source URLDirect link to the original document or transcript
DateISO 8601 (YYYY-MM-DD) publication or delivery date
Verbatim quoteExact words; no paraphrase; no truncation
Confidence score0.0–1.0 per the rubric below

Confidence Score Rubric

Every dimension is assigned a confidence score (0.0–1.0) indicating the strength of evidential support for the assessed position. This is not a measure of how confident the analyst is that the leader holds this view — it is a measure of how well the evidence supports the classification.

RangeLabelCriteriaExample
0.80–1.00HighDirect primary source; verbatim quote; on-record policy action consistent with positionBudget speech explicitly stating infrastructure-led growth priority + three corresponding capital allocation decisions
0.60–0.79ModerateTwo or more corroborating signals; at least one primary source; no major contradicting evidenceTwo speeches expressing trade dependency concern + one legislative vote consistent with the position
0.40–0.59Low–moderateSingle primary source; or multiple secondary signals; no direct contradictionSingle press conference statement without corroborating policy action
0.00–0.39LowInferred from context; no direct primary source; or significant contradicting evidence presentPosition inferred from ministry affiliation and peer group without direct statement

Historical Twin Matching

Twin matching scores a Cambodia policy signal against historical cases across five structural dimensions, each scored 0.0–1.0. The aggregate similarity score is the unweighted mean across all scored dimensions. Claude Sonnet 4.6 performs the scoring using a structured rubric prompt; reasoning is preserved in the similarity_rationale field of each match result.

Scoring dimensionWhat is compared
Economic structure similarityExport concentration, sector composition, FDI dependency ratio
Political economy similarityGovernance type, ruling coalition character, reform capacity
External vulnerability similarityPreference regime exposure, multilateral debt profile, trade partner concentration
Crisis trigger alignmentSpecific mechanism precipitating the historical case vs Cambodia's signal
Response capacity similarityInstitutional bandwidth, fiscal headroom, and reform coalition strength

A twin match scoring ≥ 0.75 is flagged HIGH RISK. A score between 0.60–0.74 is flagged MEDIUM RISK. Below 0.60, the historical case is reported but not flagged.

Conflict Detection Criteria

A conflict is flagged only when two simultaneously active positions would produce logically incoherent policy outcomes — not merely tensions or emphasis differences. The test applied is: "If both leaders' stated positions were implemented as policy today, would the resulting actions contradict each other in a way that requires one to be abandoned?"

Differences in emphasis, sequencing, or political framing are not flagged as conflicts. Timing differences are not conflicts unless both positions have the same implementation horizon and opposite directionality.

Pipeline

The full pipeline runs: scraper (fetches primary sources) → extractor (Claude API extracts structured dimension data) → twin_matcher (scores signals against historical cases) → conflict_detector (compares leader pairs) → site/build.py (generates this site). All intermediate JSON is preserved in data/. Weekly CI refreshes leader profiles where new source material is available.

Limitations

Profiles reflect publicly stated positions only. Operational doctrine visible only through classified deliberations, private communications, or unrecorded decisions is not captured. Confidence scores reflect evidential quality, not predictive certainty. The system is optimised for Cambodia's senior leadership cohort and may not generalise to other political contexts without prompt recalibration.
