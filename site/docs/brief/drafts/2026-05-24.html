◈ SITUATION ASSESSMENT

Cambodia enters this week under compounding structural pressure: the 2029 EBA deadline is now inside a three-year window, making it an operational crisis rather than a planning horizon, while the Funan Techo Canal deepens Chinese infrastructure dependency at precisely the moment Western market access is most vulnerable. Hun Manet's government retains internal cohesion through patronage continuity inherited from Hun Sen, but the economic technocrats (Pornmoniroth, Serey, Nimul) are operating with increasingly misaligned assumptions about how much reform is both possible and politically survivable. The macro numbers remain defensible — 5–6% growth projections are credible — but the structural risks beneath them are compounding faster than the reform instruments can address them.

⚠ CONFLICT ALERT

The single most dangerous internal contradiction is between **Say Sam Al's extraction-as-development model at the Ministry of Environment and Pornmoniroth's fiscal stability doctrine at MEF**, mediated through the EBA compliance requirement. The EU's EBA conditionality includes environmental governance — deforestation rates, concession transparency, and impact assessment integrity — as explicit criteria alongside labour and political standards. Say Sam Al's ministry is structurally incapable of meeting these criteria because its operational logic is patronage-driven concession approval, not regulatory enforcement. Pornmoniroth's MEF requires EBA continuity to sustain the export revenue base that underpins his fiscal framework. These two cabinet positions are therefore pulling in directly opposite directions on the single most consequential trade relationship Cambodia has. Neither official has the institutional authority or political incentive to force the other to change course, and Hun Manet has shown no indication he will impose resolution from above. The practical consequence is that Cambodia will continue to produce environmental compliance documents for EU consumption while the underlying concession regime continues unchanged — a performance of compliance that becomes harder to sustain as the 2029 deadline approaches and EU monitoring intensifies.
