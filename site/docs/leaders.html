Synthesis

  • growth_theory
    Budget priorities will continue to favour FDI-attracting infrastructure (airports, ports, SEZs) over institutional SME development. Garment sector support likely to continue until a credible alternative revenue base exists.
  • risk_tolerance
    Hun Manet is unlikely to accept short-term pain for long-term structural gain voluntarily. Policy experimentation will be gradual, consensus-based, and reversible if politically costly. Crisis responses will be fast on optics, slower on underlying structure.
  • time_horizon
    The 2029 LDC graduation problem is likely to be underplayed until late. External partners should not assume the government has an internal solution to preference loss; evidence suggests it does not.
  • dependency_assumptions
    The live stress scenario is a 'triple squeeze': US tariffs + Chinese slowdown + EBA loss. Cambodia’s BATNA is structurally weaker than its diplomatic posture suggests in any negotiation under this scenario.
  • institution_vs_relationship
    Formal institutions and agreements matter less than who is present in the decision room. The Hun family and CPP inner circle are the true decision nodes. Institutional channels are primarily for legitimation, not actual decision-making.
  • global_positioning_logic
    Cambodia will avoid choosing sides and will keep extracting value from both China and the West. The core question is not alignment but what Cambodia seeks from each engagement: usually investment, trade access, and diplomatic legitimacy.
  • consistency_score
    Foreign policy behaviour is the most reliable predictor of future actions. Domestic governance and reform commitments should be discounted until anchored by concrete budget lines or structural judicial changes.
